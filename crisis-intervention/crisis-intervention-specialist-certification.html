crisis intervention specialist certification

Comprehensive health of adolescents and youth program aims to improve access and availability of services, at all levels of the system of health care, covering current health needs of adolescents and youth, with a focus on gender and cultural relevance, in the field of promotion, prevention, treatment and rehabilitation, incorporating into their families and the community. According to the who adolescence is the period of time from 10 to 19 years approximately and can be subdivided into three: early adolescence (ages 10-13), medium (14-17), and late (17-19). The objective of the care of adolescents: Encourage their normal growth and development Promote protective behaviors and decrease risk to their health behaviours Preventing diseases and treating their health problems, in a proactive, risk approach and participatory. Get information about available benefits, and facilitate their access to these benefits in friendly, timely, differentiated services, with staff trained in adolescence, which respond to their health needs. These services must facilitate the continuity of care and guide decision-making for health and quality of life, social integration, gender equality and active. Adolescent health care must support and encourage the process of progressive autonomy.
